<Section icon={Target} title="Epic Egg Cost Breakdown and Buy Order">

The Epic Egg cost looks simple on paper, but the real decision is timing. In practice, the right buy order is: build basic income, move through Rare eggs, then save for Epic once your park can recover from the spend without going dead quiet.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Fill the starter park first">
    Use Common and Uncommon Eggs to turn empty land into income. Early dinosaurs keep visitors coming in, which makes the Epic save much easier.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Move through Rare before Epic">
    Rare Eggs at **10,000 Cash** are the clean bridge into mid-game. They create the income base that makes a **25,000 Cash** Epic purchase feel reasonable.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Save only when your park already earns well">
    Start holding Cash for Epic Eggs once your current dinosaurs, upgrades, and visitor flow can keep the park moving while you save.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Buy Epic when the next reinvestment is still possible">
    After the Epic Egg, you should still be able to buy placement, upgrades, or another egg soon after. If not, wait a little longer.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Situation | Recommended Move | Why It Works |
|---|---|---|
| Park still empty | Buy Common/Uncommon eggs | Faster income per slot |
| Rare income feels stable | Start Epic savings | Better timing for 25,000 Cash |
| You can replace weak dinos | Buy Epic Egg | Upgrade value is real |
| Cash recovery would be slow | Delay Epic purchase | Protect growth momentum |
